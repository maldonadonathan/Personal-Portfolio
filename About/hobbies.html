  • Psychological Thrillers

    Psychological thrillers and horror movies entertain the child within me as I scratch at my brother's door later that night. As I will soon be away from my family, I cherish the moments I spent with my two younger brothers watching these films. These psychological thrillers also encourage me to question various aspects of life, including what it means to deserve something. The Platform, notably, ranks amongst the highest in my personal viewing history as the plot mirrored how I view privilege and the accessibility certain groups have to resources in higher education and wealth compared to other groups who were merely born into their circumstances.

  • Amusement Parks

    Every summer, my family loves going to Magic Mountain in Valencia, California. I am known in my family to have the worst, most atrocious scream because I laugh viciously instead. My favorite ride is the Drop of Doom at night because viewing the park lights is a surreal experience. Again, I spend most of my time here with my family and close friends. The park has acted as a haven where I can release any stress from days prior.

  • Korean BBQ

    Having lived in Los Angeles, my exposure to other cultures was high. However, no meal comes close to Korean BBQ with my cousins as we laugh over our current week's stories. Despite being incapable of eating another bite, I always suggest boba milk tea immediately after and am greeted with "of course" every time.
