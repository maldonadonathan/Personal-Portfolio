Welcome, everyone, my name is Nathan Maldonado, and I am a senior at Clovis North High School and soon-to-be first-year student at Duke University. I am an FGLI (first-generation, low-income) student, and I am the oldest in my family with two younger siblings. I currently live in Fresno, California, but I previously lived in Los Angeles, California, before high school. I am incredibly family-oriented, so I spend much of my free time at various amusement parks with them or watching psychological thrillers.

Since moving to the Central Valley, I have taken an interest in artificial intelligence and have enrolled in my high school robotics team and coding club, so I intend to study computer science and linguistics at Duke. In my first year of high school, I won the Let Your Invention Fly Challenge competition hosted by NASA and California State University, Fresno. However, my interest in computer science is derived from my ethnic heritage: K'iche' Maya. K'iche' is an indigenous language spoken in Guatemala, and very few educational tools exist to preserve the language, so I intend to utilize my background in computer science and artificial intelligence to create technology that does just that. Furthermore, I encourage you to navigate toward my previous works, notably my personal statement and tourism website! Don't hesitate to contact me on LinkedIn or email if you have any concerns or are interested in hearing more about my experiences; I would love to answer all questions!

I have recently been inducted into the University Scholars Program at Duke University, a full-ride scholarship that includes a stipend. The scholarship supports students pursuing interdisciplinary fields, and my interest in computer science and linguistics was recognized. I am grateful for all the recognition I have received, and if you are interested, I invite you to learn more.

Psychological thrillers are how I spend most of my free time. I try to have a decent work-life balance, so I try to also spend time with my friends and family by going out to eat at Korean barbeque spots in downtown LA and Six Flags: Magic Mountain. However, my favorite places include Yosemite National Park, Little Tokyo, and Boyle Heights. If you're interested in how I spend my free time and getting to know myself as a person, please learn more!

In the short term, I will be graduating high school as Valedictorian and attending Duke University on a full-ride scholarship. In the long run, however, I hope to graduate with a degree in computer science and linguistics and then attend a graduate program in a similar field. Ultimately, I hope to work in artificial intelligence and create technology that preserves indigenous languages. To learn more about my plan, click below!
