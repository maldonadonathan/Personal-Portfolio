My first introduction to web development was through Chronoshift Expeditions, an imaginary company created by me that followed the storyline of Ray Bradbury's "A Sound of Thunder." In the short story, people from the future can travel to different periods, so to capture and sell the product—time travel—I began to research travel and safari websites from various competitors.

Gathering information from what my competitors would provide, I made it my goal to do one better by providing a meaningful experience and creating a pleasant user experience. As Chronoshift Expeditions was only my first project, I should note that I had not yet begun incorporating media queries or JavaScript. Instead, I relied on prototypes and wireframes I created through Figma and HTML and CSS for the actual composition of the site.

My classmates then interacted with my prototype and left me comments that I utilized to make final adjustments for a final presentation. I appreciated the encouragement I received to investigate, yet another CART Habit of Mind, the various ways in which I could produce quality work that involves not just the creation of the site but its contents.
