Boyle Heights Photoshop

One of the tools given to us students at the Center for Advanced Research and Technology (CART) was Photoshop. I was initially given assignments to learn about the program's various features and tools, and I have since used it independently.

I took the below photo in Boyle Heights, a neighborhood in East Los Angeles, California, with my girlfriend. The graffiti, background, and dirty glass made the image unattractive, and while I still loved the picture, I wanted to remove all the distracting elements.

I then utilized the patch, eyedropper, gradient, burn, and blur tools to remove the parts of the image I felt took away from my girlfriend and me. However, I was most proud of my incorporation of graffiti of our names into the picture. I, of course, did not tag "Cathy + Nathan" on the wall. Instead, I found an online font and layered that onto the original image. I then blurred the text, and I am proud of the results.
